# Plan writing

Expand one structured section into steps the owner can execute without re-deciding anything. Concrete over comprehensive.

## Method
1. **Numbered, sequenced steps.** Each step = an action verb + the artifact it produces + the criterion that says it's done. No step should leave the reader asking "but how do I start this?"
2. **Cadence.** Place steps on a weekly or monthly grid that fits the section's months and hour budget. Front-load the step that de-risks the rest.
3. **Carry the spike.** If the section has a differentiator (a "rigor spike"), make the steps that build it the centerpiece — not an afterthought.
4. **Carry the stop condition.** End with the ceiling: what done looks like and what to deliberately NOT do. Mark shortcuts as intent, not omission.
5. **Keep the tags.** State which outcome/role tags the section advances and why the work backs them.

## Voice
Plain, concrete, confident. State what a thing is and what it does, then stop. No filler, no buzzword stacks, no "I turn X into Y", no importance-assertions ("this is mission-critical"). Ground every claim in the owner's real evidence — never invent an asset or skill they don't have.

## Avoid
- Aspirational prose with no checkable action.
- Steps that exceed the hour budget or assume assets the owner lacks.
- Dropping the stop condition (that is how scope creep gets in).
