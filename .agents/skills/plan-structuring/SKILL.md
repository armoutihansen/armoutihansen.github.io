# Plan structuring

Turn a rough roadmap into a structured skeleton a writer can expand and a verifier can check. You produce structure, not prose.

## Inputs you need
- The goal and the hard deadline.
- The owner's real capacity (hours/week) and their current evidence/assets.
- The anchors (projects/threads) and any stop conditions already given.
- The taxonomy of outcome/role tags the plan optimizes for.

## Method
1. **Anchor decomposition.** For each anchor define: scope-to-done (one sentence), the milestones that prove progress, the proof surface (where the world sees it), and an explicit stop condition — the ceiling, i.e. what NOT to do.
2. **Sequence & dependencies.** Order anchors by dependency and by which bank the earliest credibility. Mark what runs in parallel vs what blocks.
3. **Capacity ledger.** Sum the hour estimates against real capacity over the horizon. If it doesn't fit, say so and cut scope — never silently overcommit. Name the single most likely slip and what protects it (the critical path).
4. **Tag every section** with the outcome/role tags it advances, so the writer and verifier can check each unit earns its place.
5. **Success criteria.** Each section gets measurable done-criteria, not vibes.

## Output
A structured outline: an overview line, the critical path, a capacity check, and a list of sections — each with id, title, months, role tags, outcome, milestones, dependencies, time budget, stop condition, and success criteria. No step-by-step prose yet.

## Avoid
- Padding the plan with sections that don't advance a tagged outcome.
- Overcommitting capacity; a plan that ignores the hour budget is fiction.
- Vague milestones ("make progress on X") — a milestone is a checkable artifact.
